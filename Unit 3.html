The Issue of Essays in Schools


Summary The purpose of an essay is to convey nuanced information compactly and professionally, but when used in school, it becomes a need to expand word count. Teachers should be aware of the grade level of their students and set reasonable goals to avoid overworking and creating uncreative work.



Information Essays have become part of everyday life, or at least that’s what school might make you think. When counting jobs that use an essay of something to a similar effect. There are very few that most people are interested and when we exclude jobs that have ‘write’ in the name we end up with undesirable office jobs. To start with, to take a stance of view for writing this we will be looking at some of my aspirations for a job. A good example to look at is networking professionals. A networking professional’s day may include receiving a request for a job, assessing the situation, and writing the scope of the project to present to the customer to assure the customer of time, cost, and interruption. Notice the use of ‘Writing the scope’, a scope is the amount of work to be done in a digestible form for someone out of the loop of whatever profession it pertains to. Scopes are typically written; however, it is typically a page or two of bullet points. The purpose of an essay is to convey nuanced information compactly and professionally. When essays are used in school to signify a standard of writing that becomes a hatred for essays, and there for writing. Many people’s styles of writing are not fit for an essay, but the apparent only way to convey any information there is a necessity to conform to a style of writing, one used for hundred-page books or studies where information is compact to a point of information, not to some randomly decided word count. Content, the content of an essay is typically new information being related to old through inference or direction, however when a school uses an essay, there becomes a need to expand word count until an arbitrary goal is met. There are two ways this is done, one is setting a goal that is reasonable with enough direction to make substantial content and the other way is when this power is abused, knowingly or not, by teachers that don’t know the grade level of their students, good direction, or/and reasonable goals. When the second type is used there become issues, namely, not knowing grade level leads to overworking for a poor grade, not knowing what the teacher is asking of them leading to incorrect wordage, and unreasonable goals, once again overworking the students for what is passable. When a student has even one of these problems it can lead to repetitive, uncreative, stressed work that only ends up being passable or not even, typically because of some arbitrary extra part that wasn’t communicated properly and so was graded poorly anyways. This is not always the issue, some teachers, however rare, are good teachers. some teachers have a topic that is fun to talk about, have well-lengthed word counts, and communicate the wants of the assignment to a high level. Teachers like this are somewhat rare while still being the most wanted. Writing in rage. Writing in rage is a ‘style’ of writing used by some to complete an essay in the arbitrary time, requirements, mal-communication, and goals a teacher had decided that they are content with. This style can sometimes be better than if the same student had done the essay normally as years of bad teachers can unknowingly promote it, only enhancing their proficiency in writing while under stress or anger. Saying all this writing in rage is not better, it can normally only be done the days following a deadline and can change tone throughout the writing. In short writing in rage can create unhealthy habits that can be introduced to a workplace and likely affect the outcome for the worst. References, references are a teacher’s way of creating extra work, when writing on an issue known, there are next to no references, and even worse when a teacher gives an open topic that can be selected to exclude references and references are part of the assignment. Many people do not like references as they break the flow of writing, especially in a text that doesn’t require any. If the topic is not always about research, then putting it into the rubric is hypocritical. The topic needs to be something of research for it to be in the rubric. Not doing this leads to references that are not connected to the essay in any meaningful way. Books are another type of reference, books typically consist of hundreds of pages, which can result in thousands of words. When using a book as a reference it is difficult to skim through it without skipping true meaning, and reading through the entire book can be time-consuming to the day, which can interfere with other classes. With all this said, the only people it affects in students, thinking from a high school point of view many other classes opportunities and extracurriculars are hindered by using essays in schools. Attempting CE classes, clubs, sports, other classes, and college classes are all hindered by an English class only assigning essays for students with large word counts that take more time than any other class.
Solution What to do to fix this. There are many ways this could be fixed, and some have been implemented to varying success. However, there is no way to make a research-backed solution to essays, but there are inferences that can be drawn from the world around us that are not all in a book of things, relating to what I have just written. There are a few ways to fix essays and the ones I believe should be effective will follow. To fix an essay it needs to know how it was assigned. An assignment for an essay will typically consist of a word count, topic, formatting, instructions, rubric, and any extra parts. The word count. One-way essays have hindered schools is the unreasonable word counts, limiting these would improve morale as there is no longer a massive assignment looming over, and increase motivation. This would also allow more time for other classes that may need time from their students to work without burnout. The topic. The topic essays have two good types, one being concise exact topics, where it is easy to grasp the topic and inferences don’t need to be made. The other way to fix a topic is to assign a subject that has a wide range. This can be something like dystopia or fantasy, where the rubric can guide the student to make the essay with the subject provided. The formatting. When addressing ‘formatting’ it is to say the instructions on how to format an essay. When there is very little instruction on how to format there are extremes that can have massive text, no title, comic sans, and more which can all be looked at as unprofessional. When a common formatting standard is shared there is much less confusion as to what the essay should look like. The rubric. Another way to confuse students is to make the rubric unclear. One example is including two seemingly contradicting rubric goals in the same essay, without proper explanation students can get frustrated and not meet all the goals. Another way essays can be confusing is when it is simplified, for example, simplifying the word prolonged into longer can have very different meanings in context while seeming the same.
The Book The book I selected was ‘The Story of My Experiments With Truth’ by Mahatma Gandhi. This book is an autobiography depicting the ongoings of Gandhi’s life. Conclusion In conclusion, to fix the issues with essays, it would be beneficial to fix what makes assigning essays difficult. word count which can be appropriate to the grade level, a topic that can either be concise or extremely open, and a rubric that can be appropriately created with the essay in mind. If all of this is adopted then essays in schools might be seen as even fun by some students, or at the very least not a massive hindrance.
